Exploratory Data Analysis and Visualization

Recording systems for human remains are based on the recovery of a more or less complete individual in an isolated context. These systems do not easily lend themselves to the fragmentary human remains that are often encountered on archaeological sites, let alone in many forensic contexts, where postmortem events, including dismemberment and subsequent animal scavenging, result in scattered, disarticulated and fragmentary human remains.

Our Strategy

Our strategy to arrive upon discernible conclusions can be broadly classified into three parts. Firstly, preparing the data by identifying what is important, cleansing it avoid redundancy and not considering data that does not add value. Data analysis is understanding the data, establishing relationships and arriving upon inferences. Data visualization is like the icing on the cake where it appeals to the viewer/user at a glance!

Data Preparation

Data Preparation is the process of collecting, cleaning, and consolidating data into one file or data table, primarily for use in analysis.

Analysis

Data analysis has two prominent methods: qualitative research and quantitative research. Each method has their own techniques.

Visualization

Data visualization is a general term that describes efforts to help people understand the significance of data by placing it in a visual context.
